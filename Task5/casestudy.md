UPI fraud is a cybercrime. Criminals trick people into sending money or sharing banking details. It happens through calls, messages or links from banks or payment companies.

* In a case a fraudster contacts a victim through phone, SMS or WhatsApp.

The fraudster pretends to be a bank officer or customer care executive.

They inform the victim about an issue like account verification or prize winnings.

The victim is asked to click a link or approve a payment request.

Sometimes they are asked for their OTP or UPI PIN.

Once the victim shares this information the transfers money from their bank account.

Students, people and those new to digital payments are usually targeted.

They may not know about online safety practices.

UPI fraud causes loss of money, stress and difficulty in recovering the stolen amount.

Victims also lose trust in payment systems.

This case study shows it is crucial to stay alert while using payment services.

Never share information, with unknown persons.

UPI fraud can be prevented by being cautious and aware of safety practices.

UPI safety is everyones responsibility.

We must be careful while making UPI transactions.
